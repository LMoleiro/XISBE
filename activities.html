Sofia Fernandes

Sofia Fernandes is an assistant professor at the Department of Physics, Faculty of Sciences of the University of Lisbon (DF FCUL) and a researcher at IBEB, with a multidisciplinary background spanning Physics, Physiotherapy, and Neurosciences. She is dedicated to model-guided strategies for optimizing non-invasive brain and spinal stimulation (NIBSS) in neural rehabilitation. Her work integrates neuromodulation, computational modelling, and neurophysiology. She currently leads the NIBSS modelling team at IBEB and collaborates internationally on brain and spinal stimulation research.

Gonçalo Cotovio

Gonçalo Cotovio completed his degree in Medicine at NOVA Medical School in 2014. In 2020 he graduated in Epidemiology and Clinical Research from the Portugal Clinical Research Training Program, Harvard Medical School. In 2024 he completed his residency in Psychiatry at Unidade Local de Saúde de Lisboa Ocidental.

He is now a full-time Psychiatrist and clinical researcher at the Champalimaud Foundation Neuropsychiatry Unit and an Assistant Professor of Neurosciences and Psychiatry at NOVA Medical School. His work focuses on the neurobiology of mood disorders and neuromodulation strategies, and he was awarded a 2023 BBRF Young Investigator Grant.

Francisco Faro Viana

He is currently pursuing a PhD in Biomedical Engineering at Instituto Superior Técnico and the Champalimaud Foundation, where his research focuses on characterizing and quantifying the effects of neuromodulation using functional MRI.

Mário J. Pinheiro (Moderator)

Mario J. Decoding Aging: From Molecules to Longevity

Wednesday, 16:00 - 17:30

📍 V02.1

Speakers: Susana Solá, Pedro Duarte Batista

Moderated by Simão Rocha

Susana Solá

Susana Solá is an Associate Professor with Habilitation at the Faculty of Pharmacy, Universidade de Lisboa, and leads the Stem Cell Bioenergetics and Neuroregeneration group at iMed.ULisboa. Her research focuses on how metabolic signals regulate neural stem cell fate and plasticity, particularly in the brain, with a strong emphasis on adult neurogenesis and its role in cognition and mental health. Her work has evolved from studying cellular stress and apoptosis to uncovering non-canonical roles of these pathways in neural stem cell differentiation. Her current research explores how metabolism, diet, and microbiome-derived metabolites influence adult neurogenesis through mitochondrial regulation, aiming to develop novel, non-invasive strategies to counteract age-related decline in neurogenesis and promote healthy brain aging.

Pedro Duarte Batista

Pedro Duarte Batista is a neurosurgeon at ULS Santa Maria, specializing in Functional and Stereotactic Neurosurgery, with a focus on deep brain stimulation, pain, and spasticity. A faculty member at the University of Lisbon, Faculty of Medicine, he teaches Neuroanatomy and Neurosurgery, bridging clinical practice with medical education. He is currently a PhD student at GIMM, in the Luísa Lopes Lab, where his research explores innovative treatments for Parkinson’s disease using optogenetics and bioluminescence. His work aims to bridge the gap between basic research and clinical application to improve therapeutic options for patients.

Simão Rocha

Simão T. da Rocha is an Assistant Professor at Instituto Superior Técnico, Universidade de Lisboa, and Head of the Stem Cell Epigenetics Lab at the Institute for Bioengineering and Biosciences. He earned his PhD from the University of Cambridge, followed by postdoctoral research at Institut Curie and training in chromatin biology at BRIC, in Copenhagen. In 2015, he joined iMM as a staff scientist, where he began applying his expertise in epigenetics to stem cell biology and human disease. Since 2022, he has led his own research group at iBB/IST-UL, focusing on integrating epigenetic principles into stem cell-based applications in developmental biology and biomedicine. In addition to leading research projects and collaborating with the European network EURAS, he has published in high-impact journals such as Nature Communications and Stem Cell Reports. He is also dedicated to teaching and mentoring the next generation of scientists at IST-UL.

Zita Martins

Zita Martins is an Astrobiologist, Associate Professor with Aggregation and Vice-President for International Affairs at Instituto Superior Técnico (IST). Her research interests include the detection of chemical signatures of extra-terrestrial life on space missions, and the potential chemical contribution of meteorites and comets to the origin of life.

She graduated in Chemistry from IST in 2002 and obtained her PhD in Astrobiology from Leiden University (The Netherlands) in 2007. She was an Invited Scientist at NASA Goddard (USA) in 2005 and 2006. She worked at Imperial College London from 2007 to 2017, where in 2009 she was awarded a University Research Fellowship worth 1 million pounds by the Royal Society. Since January 2018 she is a Professor at IST, where she created the first Astrobiology and Cosmochemistry laboratory in Portugal.

Zita Martins is co-investigator on ESA projects OREOcube and EXOcube aboard the International Space Station, a Community Scientist on the ARIEL mission (ESA), a member of the Hayabusa2 insoluble organic matter team (JAXA), and a member of the Comet Interceptor mission (ESA). In 2015 she was appointed Officer of the Military Order of Saint James of the Sword by the President of Portugal, and in 2025 was appointed Grand-Officer of the Order of Public Instruction.

She carried out postdoctoral research at Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität München and Technische Universität Dresden, in Germany, through a prestigious Marie Curie fellowship, and undertook research stays at leading institutions such as Newcastle University and the University of Copenhagen.

Francisco Santos

Leonilde Moreira (Moderator)

Leonilde Moreira is an Associate Professor in the Department of Bioengineering at Instituto Superior Técnico, coordinator of the Master's in Biotechnology, and Principal Investigator at the Institute for Bioengineering and Biosciences. Genetic Footprints of Portugal: History in Our DNA

Wednesday, 14:00 - 15:30

📍 V02.1

Speakers: Luísa Mesquita Pereira, João Tiago Brochado de Almeida

Moderator: Joana Feliciano

Luísa Mesquita Pereira

Luísa Pereira has a degree in Biology, and a Master's and PhD in Human Population Genetics. She is a group leader at i3S (Institute of Research and Innovation in Health, University of Porto), interested in using genetics to infer the past and evolution of human populations, as well as to evaluate susceptibility of human populations to complex diseases. She has mapped genes that may protect sub-Saharan African populations from presenting worse clinical phenotypes following infection by Helicobacter pylori and dengue fever virus.

Through international collaborations with anthropologists, geneticists, and clinicians, she has generated knowledge about the genetic diversity of populations across all continents. She is a regular presence in the media, organizes outreach events for the general public — including the course "Genetic Odyssey" — and is co-author of the popular science book The Portuguese Genetic Heritage: Human History Preserved in Genes.

João Tiago Brochado de Almeida

João Tiago Brochado de Almeida is a PhD candidate in Molecular and Environmental Biology at the University of Minho and University of Huddersfield, where he focuses on ancient DNA and human population genetics. His current research investigates the genomic history of Eurasian populations from the Bronze Age to the Medieval period, with particular emphasis on ancient individuals from Romania. By integrating high-throughput sequencing with advanced computational analyses, his work aims to reconstruct patterns of migration, admixture, and demographic change across key moments of human history.

He has contributed to studies on human population history in São Tomé and Príncipe, exploring the genetic legacy of slavery and ethnogenesis in the Gulf of Guinea, and has worked in cancer genomics analyzing mitochondrial DNA heteroplasmy. He is author and co-author of several peer-reviewed publications in international journals spanning ancient DNA authenticity, human population history, and genomic variation.

Joana Feliciano (Moderator)

Joana Feliciano is a researcher at the Institute of Bioengineering and Biosciences (iBB) and an Invited Assistant Professor at Instituto Superior Técnico. She earned her PhD in Biotechnology and Biosciences from IST in 2017, focusing on the virulence mechanisms of opportunistic pathogens from the Burkholderia cepacia complex. She completed international research stays at the University of Montpellier, the University of Würzburg, and the University of Zurich.

Currently, she investigates how regulatory RNAs and outer membrane vesicles contribute to antimicrobial resistance in clinically relevant opportunistic pathogens, aiming to identify new molecular targets for innovative antimicrobial strategies.

Laser Written Bioelectronics: Building Blocks for Epidermal Sensors

Monday, 11:30 - 13:00

📍 V02.2

Speaker: Tomás Pinheiro

Tomás Pinheiro

Tomás Pinheiro is a researcher working at the intersection of laser materials processing, flexible electronics, and wearable bioengineering. With a background in Biomedical Engineering and a research trajectory spanning NOVA School of Science and Technology, KAUST, and CENIMAT|i3N, he has built a distinctive expertise in Direct Laser Writing (DLW) as a sustainable, versatile platform for creating next-generation bioelectronic materials and devices. His work has introduced new methodologies for laser-engineered soft conductors, including laser-induced graphene on unconventional substrates, peel-off transfer techniques for skin-conformable electrodes, and multimaterial laser synthesis strategies for sensing and energy applications.

Throughout his PhD and postdoctoral research, Tomás has contributed to high-impact publications in journals such as Advanced Materials, ACS Nano, Materials Horizons, and Applied Physics Reviews. His work bridges fundamental materials science with practical device engineering, always with a focus on accessibility, sustainability, and real-world biomedical relevance.

Workshop description: Wearable biosensing is rapidly transforming health monitoring, sports performance, and human–machine interfaces. Participants will explore how laser-based fabrication enables rapid, low-cost production of flexible bioelectronic components for epidermal sensors. After a short introductory presentation, students will rotate through five practical modules:

Module 1: Laser synthesis of electrode conductive materials. Module 2: Electrochemical glucose sensing. Module 3: Epifluidics module for sweat transportation. Module 4: Carbon bioelectrodes for physiological monitoring. Module 5: Iontophoresis module for sweat stimulation.
